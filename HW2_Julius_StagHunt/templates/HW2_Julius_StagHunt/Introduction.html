In this experiment, you will be asked to make a series of simple choices. Another participant in this experiment will also be making similar choices. The amount of points you get in each round will depend upon both of your choices.

For each choice, there will be a table showing how your choices affect the number of points that will be received by you and the other participant. This table may change between rounds.

If you do not make a choice within 20 seconds, the computer will choose for you.

There will be a minimum of 5 rounds, and a maximum of 10 rounds. Each round will require you to make one choice.

After the first 5 rounds, at the end of each round, the computer will randomly determine whether or not the experiment will continue to another round. The probability of continuing to another round will be 9/20, which is 45%. If there is a 10th round, the experiment will end after that round.

The computer will randomly select one round to be the paid round. Any money you received in the paid round, plus {{ Constants.show_up_fee | c }} simply for participating, will be paid to you at the end of the experiment.
