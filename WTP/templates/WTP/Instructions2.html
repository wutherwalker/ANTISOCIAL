In each round, everyone will be shown one pair of goods ({{ object_1 }} vs. {{ object_2 }}). You have the option of buying one of two goods.

  1. Choose either {{ object_1 }} or {{ object_2 }} that you want to buy.
  2. Indicate the highest price you would be willing to pay for the product by moving the slider.

After you have finished, one of the prices listed the following screen will be selected at random.

If the price you indicated is equal to or greater than the randomly chosen price, then you will receive the item. {% if real %} If this occurs, you will actually get the item. Otherwise, the system will simply tell you that you would have bought it. {% else %} The system will tell you if you would have bought the item, but you will not actually get any item in these hypothetical rounds. {% endif %} You do not pay the amount you indicated on the price list. Instead, you pay the randomly chosen price, which may be lower than the price you indicated, and will certainly not be higher.

Example: if you offer {{ example_bid }} and the randomly chosen price is {{ low_price }} then you will receive the item and pay {{ low_price }}.

If your offer is less than the randomly chosen price, then you do not buy the item. Instead, you keep your money. {% if real %} Since this is a real round, you will actually get this money at the end of the experiment. {% else %} Since these are hypothetical rounds, you will see how much money you would get, but you will not get any money. {% endif %}

Example: if you offer {{ example_bid }} and the randomly chosen price is {{ high_price }}, then you will not receive the item but keep all of your money.

As a buyer, you should offer exactly the maximum amount you would be willing to pay in exchange for the item being sold. Your best strategy is to determine how much you personally value the item, and record that value as your offer. There are no advantages to indicating any other price, as making a lower offer will not lower the price you pay.
