The American Cancer Society is a nationwide, community-based, voluntary health organization dedicated to eliminating cancer as a major health problem by preventing cancer, saving lives, and diminishing suffering from cancer, through research, education, advocacy, and service. The American Cancer Society award grants to academic institutions and professionals in the fields of medicine and science for investigations into the causes, prevention, and cure of cancer. Researchers at the American Cancer Society analyze trends in cancer occurrence and risk factors, perform screening, and oversee behavioral studies on areas such as family dynamics, minority issues, and communication between physicians and patients.
